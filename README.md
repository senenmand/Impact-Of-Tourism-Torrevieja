# Impact of Tourism in Torrevieja

![Foto de Torrevieja](images/sky_photo.jpg)

## Project Overview

This repository contains a research project on the impact of tourism in **Torrevieja**.  
The study focuses on key territorial, demographic, and environmental dynamics linked to tourism pressure in a coastal city context.

## Project Objective

To analyze the effects of tourism in Torrevieja through four core challenges that are critical for planning, governance, and long-term sustainability.

## The 4 Challenges Addressed

### 1) Estimating the Real Population

One of the main challenges is measuring Torrevieja’s **real (effective) population**, beyond official census figures.  
Because of second homes, temporary residents, and tourist flows, the population present in the city often differs from registered population data, affecting planning and resource allocation.

### 2) Seasonality

Tourism activity is highly seasonal, with major peaks during specific periods of the year.  
This seasonality creates fluctuations in demand for services, mobility, infrastructure, and employment, making stable year-round planning more difficult.

### 3) Tourist Housing (Viviendas de Uso Turístico)

The growth of **tourist-use housing** is a key issue in the local tourism model.  
This phenomenon impacts residential availability, rental dynamics, neighborhood structure, and urban regulation, requiring clearer governance and monitoring tools.

### 4) Environmental Sustainability

Tourism intensity generates pressure on natural resources and urban ecosystems.  
The project addresses environmental sustainability as a central challenge, including resource use, waste generation, and the need to protect the local coastal environment while maintaining economic activity.

## Conclusion

Tourism is a strategic pillar for Torrevieja, but it also creates complex management challenges.  
The study concludes that improving decision-making requires better measurement of the real population, stronger responses to seasonality, clearer regulation of tourist housing, and firm environmental sustainability policies.
